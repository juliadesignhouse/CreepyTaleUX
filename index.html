Gesture & Area in Screen

The non-user research highlights that using the number of fingers is crucial to deciding on the gaming experience in a mobile game.

Using one or two fingers on each hand is ideal for effective accessibility and engagement when playing a mobile game. While single-finger gestures are recommended for ease of execution.

For the iPhone version of Creepy Tale, this translates to using one-finger mechanics for every system and interaction with the game, optimizing simplicity and reducing player frustration.

The gamer stance is a control style where players hold the device with both hands and use their thumbs for input, providing intuitive and precise control for actions and traversal.

Control Design

The control design will mainly focus on the traversal system, inventory system, map, and different interactive actions:

Left Thumb Control:

Traversal System:

Movement: A control scheme is positioned on the left bottom of the screen, aligning with gaming controllers. This control allows characters to move left and right.

Jump: The jump button is placed at the top of the movement but still within the easy accessible zone.

Right Thumb Control:

Inventory: An inventory placed at the bottom right, with a collapsible menu that players can expand when tapping on the inventory.

Map: Specifically designed for the mobile version, the map allows players to toggle between a single-room view and a multi-room view, enabling seamless switching between a compact and expanded layout.

Actions:

Five gestures are primarily used in 1TG games: drag, hold, slide, swipe, and tap.

    These gestures are linked to two types of actions:
  • Controlled Actions: Require continuous interaction (e.g., dragging to draw or holding to steer).
  • Initiated Actions: Triggered by a single input and execute automatically or instantly (e.g., tapping to attack or swiping to move).

Tap: Use: Interacting with objects or performing actions. For instance:

Tapping to pick up an item.

Tapping on a door to open or close it.

Tapping to activate a hidden mechanism, there will be an icon to remind the user to intuitively guide the user tap on it.
